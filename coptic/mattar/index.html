THE FIRST PERFECT

‎التام الماضى

The FIRST PERFECT, also labelled in some texts as "the simple past," is the special narrative past tense, (I did);

In addition and to a lesser extent, it relates to the English perfect (I have written … etc.).

الماضى التام يعبر عن الزمن التاريخى الذى تم حدوثه وهو من أكثر الأزمنة إستعمالا فى القبطية‎ ٠

To understand it, let us look at some English expressions:

1- I go

2- I shall go

3- He went

4- I have gone

5- to go

If you look at the above 5 examples, they share one thing in common: all verbs include the word "go."

Each sentence of the first 4 expresses a "tense."

The last sentence expresses a mode; the "infinitive."

To Express a "Tense" in English:

FIRST, you use a pronoun; viz. I, you, we, … etc.

This will be the subject.

Then you use the verb, in the form appropriate to the tense. In this example we use "went" to express the Simple Past.

So we have changes in the structure of the verb to:

(a) express the tense; and (b) to add a personal pronoun before the verb.
