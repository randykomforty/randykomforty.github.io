Now, how do you know the difference between a plural and a singular if the noun has only one form for both the singular and plural?

The answer is through the article that is associated with the noun. It will tell you the difference. (See below)

The Definite Article

As you know, the definite article in the English language is the very simple "the". You can say man or the man.

The same article can also be used for the feminine noun. So you can say the woman.

Not only that, but the same definite article can be used for the singular form and the plural form, so you can say: the man or the men.

And the same can be used for the plural of the feminine nouns.

Now, if you can read Arabic, the following paragraph will discuss the use of the definite article in Arabic grammar.

Rule:

The Coptic language respects:

1- The gender, so there is a certain form for the single masculine and another form for the single feminine.

2- The plural form is applied for both the masculine and the feminine.

A question in your mind will always come up: Are there any rules that will make you use one or the other under different circumstances? And if yes, what are they?

The answer to this question is yes. BUT now you only have to study one rule (which actually is the most important). As for the other rules, do not bother with their studying them now. You will need them as you continue to know more about how the Coptic is written.

Rules for Single Nouns

1- Use the ⲡⲓ and the ϯ as a general denominator for the definite articles. They both can be used for any noun with very few exeptions.

Some authorities consider their use to emphasize the single identity of the noun in question. As an example, the Coptic word for "God" is "ⲛⲟⲩϯ = Noo-tee". If you say "ⲡⲓ-ⲛⲟⲩϯ", that will mean the only God that you and I know. In this example, there is an emphasis on identity.

2- As a general rule, and there are many exceptions, you use the light forms of the definite articles, i.e. [ⲫ̀, ⲑ̀], if the noun that you want to tag starts with any of the following letters:

ⲃ, ⲓ, ⲣ, ⲛ, ⲗ, ⲙ
